Data Analysis of Results

Note: Students made a mistake, and had high school students also fill this out. So the demographic is actually 15-25

Here are the key findings from the survey data represented as graphs:

  1. Age Distribution:
    • The age distribution shows a relatively even split among the three age groups (18-20, 21-23, 24-25).
  1. Gender Distribution:
    • The gender distribution is diverse, with female respondents being the most represented
  1. Education Level Distribution:
    • Respondents have varied educational backgrounds, with an equal distribution among those with a high school diploma, a bachelor's degree, and other education levels.
  1. Current Employment Status:
    • The majority of respondents are either employed part-time or unemployed, with a smaller proportion being students.
  1. Awareness of Line Worker Career:
    • More respondents are unaware of the line worker career than those who are aware of it.
  1. Perception of Line Work:
    • The perception of line work is mostly positive, with a majority rating it as 4 on a scale of 1 to 5.
  1. Consideration of Line Worker Career:
    • Most respondents are uncertain about considering a line worker career, with a few outright rejecting it.

    Deeper Analysis and Insights

    1. Cross-tabulation of Awareness and Consideration of Line Worker Career

    Insights:

    • Those unaware of the line worker career have a higher tendency to say "Yes" or "Maybe" when considering the career, but also a significant number saying "No."
    • Awareness doesn't strongly correlate with a positive consideration; rather, it shows mixed responses.

    2. Influence of Education Level on Perception and Consideration

    • Perception by Education Level:
      • Individuals with a Bachelor's degree tend to have a higher perception rating of the line work career.
      • Those with "Other" education levels show more variability in their perception.
    • Consideration by Education Level:
      • Respondents with a Bachelor's degree are more likely to consider the line worker career ("Maybe").
      • Those with "Other" education levels show a higher tendency to say "No" or "Maybe."

    3. Current Employment Status and Its Impact on Career Consideration

    Insights:

    • Students and unemployed individuals show a higher likelihood of considering the line worker career ("Maybe").
    • Employed part-time individuals are more evenly distributed across all consideration options.

    4. Correlation Analysis

    Correlation Matrix Insights:

    • There is a moderate positive correlation between "Perception of Line Work" and "Consider Line Worker Career" (0.48). This indicates that a higher perception of line work is somewhat associated with a greater likelihood of considering it.
    • "Current Employment Status" and "Consider Line Worker Career" show a weak negative correlation (-0.27), suggesting that being currently employed (especially part-time) slightly reduces the likelihood of considering a line worker career.
    • "Aware of Line Worker Career" has a weak negative correlation with "Consider Line Worker Career" (-0.21), indicating that awareness alone does not significantly drive consideration.

    Recommendations

    Based on the deeper analysis, here are some targeted recommendations:

    1. Targeted Information Campaigns:
      • Focus on providing detailed information to those currently unaware of the line worker career, emphasizing the benefits and addressing the common concerns such as physical demands and safety.
    1. Leverage Education Channels:
      • Utilize educational institutions, especially colleges, to provide workshops and seminars about the line worker career, highlighting success stories and potential growth opportunities.
    1. Tailored Messaging for Different Employment Statuses:
      • For students and unemployed individuals, emphasize the stability and growth opportunities in the line worker career.
      • For part-time employed individuals, showcase the benefits of transitioning to a full-time, stable career with potential for advancement.
    1. Enhance Perception through Positive Testimonials:
      • Use testimonials from current line workers to improve the perception of the career, focusing on job satisfaction, safety measures, and work-life balance.

    By implementing these strategies, it may be possible to better address the concerns and motivations of young adults in Kansas City regarding the line worker career, ultimately increasing interest and consideration.
